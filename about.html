About Me!

My name is Griffin Faecher, and I am a rising senior at Tufts Universtiy studying computer engineering with a minor in computer science. I have a passion for embedded systems, computer architecture, and the alto saxophone. I am actively seeking opportunities to apply my growing knowledge and skills in a professional setting. I have a strong foundation in programming languages such as C, C++, and VHDL, and am eager to contribute to teams across a plethora of fields regarding computer engineering.

I have extensive professional experience in engineering roles at TRC Companies, where I participated in projects across multiple diciplines. I began as an environmental intern where i assisted in the collection and analysis of data for environmental cleanup projects. Next summer, I transitioned to a more technical role as a geospatial intern. I annotated power station imagery for the YOLO object detection model, and wrote shell scripts to automate the data collection process.

I have also been involved in a number of positions at Tufts. I was an undergraduate research assistant for Professor Paul Simmonds, where i assisted in the analysis of III-V semiconductors. I was also a teaching assistant for Tufts's Digital Logic Design course, where I ran weekly lab sessions to teach students how to utilize VHDL to design a large scale final project. I was also a teaching assistant for Tufts's Embedded Systems course, where I taught students how to use C to program an STM-Nucleo microcontroller to interact with a plethora of different peripherals.

As I come to my final year of my undergraduate studies, I am excited to explore opportunities in embedded systems, computer architecture, and whatever lies in between. Please, feel free to reach out via email or LinkedIn!
